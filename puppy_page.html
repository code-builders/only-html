Rottweiler Labrador Mix

These dogs are intelligent, observant, and make for good companions. They can be protective as well so they need to be trained not to be aggressive. If it is given the opportunity to socialize with people and other dogs when still a puppy, it will very likely accept other people and other dogs once it matures.

Also Known As...

Rottweiler labradors are also known by these names:

  • Labrottie
  • Rottador
  • Labweiler

Physical Traits

These dogs usually resemble a Rottweiler in color and build, along with having the Rottweiler's strength and stamina. The head is more similar to a labrador's in that the head and snout are more narrow. The labrador's fun-loving and energetic personality are also a common attribute given to these mixes. They have a short, stiff coat of hair which can be easily maintained.

Possible Problems

Both Rottweilers and Labradors can be affected by hip or elbow dysplasia, in addition to other joint problems like arthritis, which then can be passed on to the pups, creating the possibility of serious physical problems. When getting this mix, it is important to make sure that the parent dogs are healthy and not suffering from any of the above conditions.
