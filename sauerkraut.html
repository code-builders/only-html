Sauerkraut

Fermented foods have a long history in many cultures, with sauerkraut being one of the most well-known instances of traditional fermented moist cabbage side dishes. It is a source of vitamins C, B, and K; the fermentation process increases the bioavailability of nutrients rendering sauerkraut even more nutritious than the original cabbage.

List of Ingredients

  • Medium/large cabbages - 2
  • Medium carrots - 2-3
  • Fine sea salt - 2 Tbsp
  • Sugar - 1 Tbsp

Preparation steps

  1. Remove the outermost leaves of the cabbage, cut it into quarters and shred the cabbage
  2. Shred the carrots
  3. In a large bowl, place cabbage, carrots; add sea salt and sugar. Knead it together really well for the juice to be released from the cabbage (4-5 min)
  4. Fill a glass jar with the cabbage mixture and pack it in very tightly so that the juice from the cabbage covers the cabbage completely. You want a jar big enough to be filled only about 2/3 full so it has room to expand.
  5. Make a press over the top of the cabbage by pushing down with a plate. Top with a small jar of water, a super clean rock or whatever else would make a good weight. Place the lid on the jar but do not tighten. Let stand at room temp for 4 days or until sour. It will stop fermenting when it's done.

To serve, drain the amount of cabbage that you would like to eat by firmly squeezing out the excess juice with your hands.
